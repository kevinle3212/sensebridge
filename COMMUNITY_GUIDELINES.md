# Community Guidelines

SenseBridge is an accessibility project. That shapes how this community works,
not just what the code does.

- **Accessibility regressions are taken seriously.** A feature that looks
  fine sighted but breaks VoiceOver is not a minor issue — see
  [`docs/accessibility.md`](docs/accessibility.md) and the accessibility-impact
  section required in every pull request.
- **Lived expertise outranks abstract opinions.** This community includes
  blind, low-vision, deaf, and deaf-blind people. When a disabled contributor
  or tester says something doesn't work for them, that report outweighs a
  sighted contributor's intuition about how it "should" work. Design and
  implementation debates defer to the people who actually use assistive
  technology daily.
- **Be direct and be kind.** See [`CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)
  for the baseline standard of behavior this extends.
- **"Good first issue" labels welcome newcomers**, especially
  accessibility-minded ones who may not otherwise think of themselves as
  "engineers who contribute to open source."

## Getting involved beyond code

GitHub has run accessibility-focused open-source initiatives — a Global
Accessibility Awareness Day (GAAD) pledge, accessibility summits, and
assistive-technology hackathons. These are worth engaging once the project
has something concrete to show, both for visibility and for finding
contributors and testers who bring real assistive-technology expertise.
